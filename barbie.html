Barbie

★★★★☆ 7.5/10
2023 • Comedy/Fantasy • 1h 54m

Director: Greta Gerwig

Cast: Margot Robbie, Ryan Gosling, America Ferrera

Available on: Max

Review

Greta Gerwig's "Barbie" is a surprisingly profound and witty exploration of feminism, identity, and human nature, cleverly disguised as a pink-saturated comedy. The film follows Stereotypical Barbie (Margot Robbie) as she experiences an existential crisis that leads her to venture into the real world, accompanied by Ken (Ryan Gosling).

Margot Robbie perfectly embodies Barbie's journey from plastic perfection to genuine self-discovery, while Ryan Gosling steals scenes as a hilariously insecure Ken grappling with his own identity crisis. America Ferrera delivers a powerful monologue about the contradictions of womanhood that serves as the film's emotional core.

Technical Aspects

The production design by Sarah Greenwood is a triumph, creating a vibrant Barbie Land that feels both artificial and tangible. The costume design by Jacqueline Durran is exceptional, paying homage to Barbie's fashion history while adding contemporary flair. The soundtrack, featuring original songs and carefully chosen pop hits, enhances the film's playful yet meaningful tone.

Gerwig's direction balances comedy with social commentary, using clever visual gags and sharp dialogue to address serious themes about gender roles and societal expectations.

Verdict

Barbie succeeds as both entertainment and social commentary, delivering laughs while tackling substantial themes about identity, gender roles, and human connection. While some viewers might find the messaging heavy-handed at times, the film's charm, humor, and visual creativity make it a unique and memorable experience.

Pros

  • Clever writing and social commentary
  • Outstanding production design
  • Strong performances from the entire cast
  • Excellent soundtrack and visuals

Cons

  • Message can feel heavy-handed at times
  • Some jokes may not land for all audiences
  • Third act pacing issues
